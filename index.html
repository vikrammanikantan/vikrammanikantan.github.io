About Me

I am currently a senior at Northwestern University studying physics and computer science

I was born and raised in Singapore. After completing my high school degree at United World College of South East Asia, I moved to Chicago to pursue my undergraduate degree at Northwestern University. I am majoring in physics with a concentration in astronomy, and pursuing a minor in computer science. After graduating in March of 2022, I will be starting my PhD in astrophysics the upcoming fall.

Research

High-Energy Computational Astrophysics

My current research interests include black holes, accretion disks, angular momentum transport, collapsars and relativistic jets. I have been a part of Dr. Sasha Tchekhovskoy's research group since the summer of 2019. We use the GPU-accelerated three dimensional magnetohydrodynamic simulation code H-AMR to study black hole accretion in a variety of contexts. Below are some of the projects I have taken part in.

Angular Momentum Transport

We evolved a torus around a black hole up to 120,000 rg/c, where the accretion disk formed becomes magnetically arrested. The black hole spin is fixed at 0.9 and the initial disk thickness (h/r) was 0.03. The contours describes the log of the density with the color map shown on the right of the graph. Overplotted are the streamlines show the direction of angular momentum transport within the accretion disk, winds and jet. Paper coming soon...
