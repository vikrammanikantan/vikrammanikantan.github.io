I am a computational astrophysicist and PhD student at Steward Observatory, at the University of Arizona. I study the multi-messenger nature of compact objects such as binary black holes, neutron stars, supernovae, and other high-energy transients, using numerical tools such as IllinoisGRMHD, H-AMR, and Athena++.

Co-organizer and instructor for COMPASS, a computational and mathematical workshop for incoming undergraduates that aims to remove barriers to entering STEM through technical training.

Graduate Student Lead for Steward Observatory's new social media initiative which includes creating and running our Instagram, Twitter, and Facebook accounts, as well as developing content to post three times a week.
